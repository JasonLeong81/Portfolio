Data Science and Machine Learning

As a credit risk data scientist, I devise strategies to create an impact for my company such that KPIs are met without unnecessary costs. Devising a strategy takes time and loads of efforts as we'd have to understand the data and determine what we want to do with it. Many times I like to work bidirectionally, that is, from technical details to business metrics as well as from business metrics back to technical details. It may seem important to always start from the business point of view and work backwards, but for me, I like to take into account the overheads of technical implementations. If a devised strategy is making it too complicated to technically implement, and only small marginal gains will be a result of it, then I'd generally opt for another strategy or find a better metric given the current situation and time. As a data scientist, I think it is crucial to have good intuition (then search for data evidence later) not just about what kinds of metrics we should use but more importantly the feasibility of using it at any given situation and time. If building a model doesn't give us a significant forecasted improvement in our KPIs, then don't release it. Similarly, if using simple hard rules can already help us achieve 80% of the target, we should prioritise maintaining those rules but also be sure to check on their validity from time to time.
